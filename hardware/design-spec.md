# IMSPI 8080 controller board — design spec

Everything needed to lay the board out in KiCad and generate the manufacturing
files. Pair this with `bom.csv`. The BOM is upload-ready; the Gerbers and CPL
come out of the layout step described here.

## Board

- Outline: ~220 mm × 55 mm (wide strip covering the LED field only; switches are off-board on the bezel below). Sits
  behind the bezel, component side facing the bezel.
- Stackup: 2-layer FR-4, 1.6 mm, HASL (ENIG optional for the HTSSOP pads).
- Mounting: 6× M2.5 to short bezel standoffs (see "Mounting holes" below).
- Assembly: single-sided SMT (everything on the component/front side) to hold
  cost — toggles are off-board (wired), so nothing needs the back side.

## Mounting holes

6× M2.5, NPTH, ~2.9 mm drill, electrically isolated (single-point ground kept
clean). Each gets a ~6 mm keep-out ring — no traces, pours, or parts inside it,
so the screw head/standoff base has room. Positions in mm from the board's
top-left datum (same datum as the LED grid), chosen to sit in LED-free zones:

| Ref | X    | Y   | Zone                     |
|-----|------|-----|--------------------------|
| H1  | 7    | 7   | top-left corner margin   |
| H2  | 140  | 7   | top, inter-block gap     |
| H3  | 213  | 7   | top-right corner margin  |
| H4  | 7    | 48  | bottom-left corner       |
| H5  | 140  | 48  | bottom, inter-block gap  |
| H6  | 213  | 48  | bottom-right corner      |

The two mid-span holes (H2/H5) live in the 40 mm gap between the left LED block
(ends x=120) and the status block (starts x=160) — that keeps a 220 mm strip
from bowing without crowding any lamp. Standoffs: short, **3–5 mm** brass, so the
LEDs sit just behind the bezel windows. Nothing on the component side between
board and bezel should exceed the standoff height except the LEDs — keep the tall
parts (USB-C, microSD, JST) at the edges, which the layout already does.

## Power tree

Two USB-C inputs, grounds bonded, 5 V rails separate.

```
USB-C #1 (J1)  VBUS 5V ──► U8 AMS1117-3.3 ──► 3V3_LOGIC ──► RP2040 IOVDD/USB/ADC,
   │                                                          TLC5947 VCC (logic),
   │ D+/D- (27R series) ──► RP2040 USB                        74HC165 VCC, SD VDD,
   │ CC1/CC2 ──► 5.1k to GND                                  pull-ups
   └ GND ──────────────────────────────┐
                                        ├── common GND
USB-C #2 (J2)  VBUS 5V ──► U9 AMS1117-3.3 ──► 3V3_LED ──► 28x LED anodes
   │ CC1/CC2 ──► 5.1k to GND            │
   └ GND ───────────────────────────────┘   (LED cathodes sink into TLC5947 → GND)
```

- RP2040 core (1.1 V) is generated by its internal LDO — just decouple DVDD.
- 3V3_LED at 3.3 V (not 5 V) keeps TLC5947 dissipation low: ~1.3 V drop/channel
  instead of ~3 V. If you ever want brighter lamps off the Pi's budget, J2 is a
  dedicated feed so the LED current never touches the logic side.
- Bulk 10 µF on each of: 3V3_LOGIC, 3V3_LED, and VBUS(J1). 100 nF at every IC.

## Net / connection map (RP2040 GPIO)

Dedicated-function pins (USB, QSPI, XIN/XOUT, SWD) per the RP2040 reference
design. Peripheral GPIO assignment:

| GPIO   | Net            | To                              |
|--------|----------------|---------------------------------|
| GP2    | TLC_SCLK       | U3/U4 SCLK (SPI0 SCK)           |
| GP3    | TLC_SIN        | U3 SIN (SPI0 TX); U3 SOUT→U4 SIN|
| GP4    | TLC_XLAT       | U3/U4 XLAT                      |
| GP5    | TLC_BLANK      | U3/U4 BLANK                     |
| GP6    | TLC_GSCLK      | U3/U4 GSCLK (PWM)               |
| GP7    | HC_PLOAD       | U5/U6 SH/LD                     |
| GP8    | HC_CLK         | U5/U6 CLK                       |
| GP9    | HC_QH          | U6 QH (chain output)            |
| GP10   | SD_SCK         | U7 CLK (SPI1 SCK)               |
| GP11   | SD_MOSI        | U7 CMD (SPI1 TX)                |
| GP12   | SD_MISO        | U7 DAT0 (SPI1 RX)               |
| GP13   | SD_CS          | U7 DAT3/CS (SPI1 CSn)           |
| GP14-22| spare          | broken out to J5 area / testpoints |

TLC5947 drivers share one SPI0 daisy-chain; the two 74HC165s share a bit-banged
chain; the SD card is on its own SPI1 so a card access can't stall lamp refresh.

### LED channel map (into the TLC5947 chain)

- U3 (device 0): ch0-7 = addr A15..A8, ch8-15 = addr A7..A0, ch16-23 = data D7..D0
- U4 (device 1): ch24-27 = status 0..3 (ch28-47 spare)

### Switch header pinout

J3 — data bank (S10B-XH-A), MTS-102 SPDT ON-ON, each common → 74HC165 input,
throws bussed to 3V3/GND at the bezel (no pull-ups needed):

```
1:3V3  2:SW0  3:SW1  4:SW2  5:SW3  6:SW4  7:SW5  8:SW6  9:SW7  10:GND
```

J4 — command bank (S6B-XH-A), MTS-123 center-off momentary, each common →
74HC165 input, one throw bussed to GND at the bezel; **pull-ups on-board** (R
group) define the released state:

```
1:GND  2:CMD0  3:CMD1  4:CMD2  5:CMD3  6:spare
```

Both are side-entry (right-angle) so the wire housings mate parallel to the
board — place them along the bezel-facing edge, openings outward.

## Layout plan

1. LEDs first, locked to the bezel grid from `docs/panel-layout.md` (3 rows of 8
   over the 8 data columns + 4 status), 15 mm column pitch (aligns lamps over toggles). Their positions are
   fixed by the bezel; everything else routes around them.
2. RP2040 + flash + crystal as a tight cluster (follow the reference layout;
   crystal and QSPI traces short). TLC5947s near the LED field to keep the 28
   LED-cathode traces short. 74HC165s near J3/J4.
3. USB-C J1 and J2 at accessible board edges (Pi cable to J1; LED supply to J2).
   microSD U7 at an edge with the slot opening reachable through a bezel cutout.
4. J3/J4 right-angle headers along the switch-facing edge.
5. Star the two 5 V rails; single solid ground pour bonding both USB-C grounds.

## Design rules (JLCPCB 2-layer, economy-safe)

- Trace/space ≥ 0.2 mm (0.15 mm capable if needed); LED-cathode and signal nets 0.25 mm.
- Via 0.3 mm drill / 0.6 mm pad; annular ring ≥ 0.13 mm.
- Board-edge clearance ≥ 0.3 mm; silkscreen ≥ 0.15 mm.
- Add fiducials (3× on the assembled side) for the pick-and-place.

## Manufacturing outputs (what to upload)

Ordered at **jlcpcb.com** ("Order Now" / Add Gerber). Three files:

1. **Gerbers ZIP** — from KiCad: Plot (all copper, mask, silk, edge cuts) +
   Generate Drill Files, zipped. *(produced by the layout step)*
2. **BOM CSV** — `hardware/bom.csv` in this repo. *(ready now)*
3. **CPL / pick-and-place CSV** — KiCad → Fabrication Outputs → Component
   Placement (.pos), columns Designator/Mid X/Mid Y/Rotation/Layer.
   *(produced by the layout step)*

At upload, toggle SMT Assembly, choose the assembled side (top), and step through
component matching — confirm each part turns green; watch for any LED or socket
flagged **Standard Only** (forces Standard PCBA) and swap if you want Economic.
Verify rotations in the placement preview (pin-1 dots) before paying.
